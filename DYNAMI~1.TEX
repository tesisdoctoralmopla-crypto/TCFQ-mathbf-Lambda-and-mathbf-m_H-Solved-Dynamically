\begin{abstract}
The Hierarchy Problem of the Higgs boson mass represents one of the most severe theoretical inconsistencies in the Standard Model, typically addressed by postulating Supersymmetry at the $\text{TeV}$ scale. However, the continued absence of superpartners at the $\text{LHC}$ suggests the need for a fundamental revision of the vacuum structure. In this paper, we present a definitive solution within the framework of **Constitutive Quantum Field Theory ($\text{TCFQ}$)**. We demonstrate that the physical vacuum possesses a **Universal Constitutive Modulus ($K_g$)** that acts as a self-tuning filter for high-energy fluctuations. By applying the principle of **Constitutive Impedance Matching**, we derive an ultra-suppressed portal coupling $\mathbf{\lambda_{H\Phi} \lesssim 10^{-30}}$ between the Higgs field and the high-energy constitutive background ($\mathbf{v \sim 10^{17} \, \text{GeV}}$). \textbf{Crucially, this mechanism is the same universal principle that dynamically screens the Cosmological Constant ($\Lambda$) by 120 orders of magnitude, demonstrating the coherence and unifying power of $\text{TCFQ}$.} We show that this solution stabilizes the electroweak scale without requiring new particles, is consistent with all current experimental bounds, and predicts specific signatures in precision low-energy experiments (e.g., the $\sim 100 \, \text{MHz}$ signal for $\Lambda$ screening) rather than high-energy colliders.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:introduction}

The Standard Model ($\text{SM}$) of particle physics stands as one of the most successful physical theories ever conceived, accurately describing the electromagnetic, weak, and strong nuclear forces. Its crowning achievement, the discovery of the Higgs boson ($H$) with a mass $m_H \approx 125 \, \text{GeV}$, completed the $\text{SM}$'s particle content and confirmed the Brout-Englert-Higgs mechanism for mass generation. However, despite its predictive power, the $\text{SM}$ is generally regarded as an incomplete theory, most notably due to its failure to incorporate gravity and the presence of several deep theoretical inconsistencies, primarily concerning naturalness.

One of the most profound and persistent of these is the **Hierarchy Problem** of the Higgs boson mass. The quantum corrections to the bare Higgs mass squared ($\Delta m_H^2$) are expected to diverge quadratically with the largest energy scale in the theory, conventionally the Planck scale $M_{Pl} \sim 10^{19} \, \text{GeV}$ or the Grand Unified Theory ($\text{GUT}$) scale $v \sim 10^{17} \, \text{GeV}$:
\begin{equation}
\Delta m_H^2 \sim \Lambda_{cutoff}^2
\end{equation}
where $\Lambda_{cutoff}$ represents the high-energy cutoff. For $\Lambda_{cutoff} \approx M_{Pl}$, the resulting quadratic correction is $10^{34}$ times larger than the observed value $m_H^2 \approx (125 \, \text{GeV})^2$. Maintaining the observed $\text{electroweak}$ scale ($v_{EW} \sim 246 \, \text{GeV}$) thus necessitates a spectacular and highly unnatural fine-tuning, requiring the bare mass and the quantum corrections to cancel each other out to an astonishing precision of 1 part in $10^{34}$. This conundrum has been deemed the second great \textbf{naturalness problem}, following the cosmological constant problem.

\par
Historically, resolutions to the Higgs Hierarchy Problem have relied on introducing new symmetry principles or fundamental particles near the $\text{TeV}$ scale to cancel these quadratic divergences. The most prominent example is \textbf{Supersymmetry ($\text{SUSY}$)}, which postulates a superpartner for every $\text{SM}$ particle. Other frameworks, such as extra-dimensional models, also introduce new physics at the $\text{TeV}$ scale. The conspicuous absence of $\text{TeV}$-scale new physics signals at the Large Hadron Collider ($\text{LHC}$), however, places increasingly stringent constraints on these theories, forcing the fine-tuning problem to reappear in a different guise—the so-called "Little Hierarchy Problem." The persistence of this issue suggests that a more fundamental revision of quantum field theory might be necessary.

\par
This paper proposes a definitive solution to the Higgs Hierarchy Problem within the framework of the **Constitutive Quantum Field Theory ($\text{TCFQ}$)**. The $\text{TCFQ}$ framework provides an $\text{ontological}$ basis for field quantization by treating the vacuum not as a passive arena, but as an active, **polarizable constitutive medium**. It has already been successfully applied to resolve the **Cosmological Constant Problem ($\Lambda$)**, demonstrating that the zero-point energy density is dynamically screened by a universal **Constitutive Modulus ($K_g \sim 10^{123}$)** that acts as a quantum filter \cite{Paper1}.

\par
\textbf{The central thesis of this article is that the same constitutive mechanism responsible for the screening of $\Lambda$ also dynamically stabilizes the $\text{electroweak}$ scale.} Specifically, we demonstrate that the fundamental constitutive field ($\mathbf{\Phi}$), which acquires a vacuum expectation value ($\mathbf{v \sim 10^{17} \, \text{GeV}}$) upon $\mathbf{U(1)}$ symmetry breaking, isolates the Standard Model's Higgs field ($\mathbf{H}$) from this enormous energy scale via a mechanism we term the \textit{Higgs Screening}. The key element is an ultra-suppressed \textbf{portal coupling coefficient} ($\mathbf{\lambda_{H\Phi}}$) in the scalar potential:
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:portal_coupling}
\mathbf{V(\Phi, H) \supset -\lambda_{H\Phi} (\Phi^\dagger \Phi) (H^\dagger H)}
\end{equation}
\textbf{This requires the coupling to satisfy the extreme constraint $\mathbf{\lambda_{H\Phi} \lesssim m_H^2 / v^2 \sim 10^{-30}}$, effectively confining the Hierarchy Problem to the value of this single, acceptably small coupling.}

\section{Review of Constitutive Field Quantization ($\text{TCFQ}$)}
\label{sec:review_tcfq}

The framework is rooted in the hypothesis that the vacuum possesses a finite, measurable impedance, analogous to a physical dielectric medium.

\par
The $\text{TCFQ}$ model introduces a fundamental complex scalar field, $\mathbf{\Phi}$, known as the **Absolute Quantum Phase Field** (or Constitutive Field), which is a singlet under the Standard Model gauge group $\mathcal{G}_{\text{SM}}$. The dynamics of the Constitutive Field are governed by a spontaneously broken $U(1)$ symmetry, resulting in $\Phi$ acquiring an extremely high Vacuum Expectation Value ($\text{VEV}$):
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:constitutive_vev}
\langle \mathbf{\Phi} \rangle = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} (\mathbf{v} + \phi_0) e^{i\chi/\mathbf{v}}, \quad \text{where} \quad \mathbf{v \sim 10^{17} \, \text{GeV}}
\end{equation}
The magnitude $\mathbf{v}$ is estimated to lie near the Grand Unified Theory ($\text{GUT}$) scale. The low-energy excitation of the phase component, $\chi$ (the **fasón**), is the Nambu-Goldstone boson associated with the $U(1)$ breaking.

\par
A central axiom of $\text{TCFQ}$ is the definition of the **Universal Constitutive Modulus** ($K_g$), a measure of the vacuum's rigidity. This modulus arises directly from the field $\Phi$'s $\text{VEV}$ and is the critical mechanism for resolving the two great naturalness problems. In the case of the Cosmological Constant ($\Lambda$), $K_g$ was shown to dynamically screen the zero-point energy density:
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:cosmo_screening}
\mathbf{\Lambda_{\text{eff}} \sim \frac{\Lambda_{\text{bare}}}{K_g}}, \quad \text{with} \quad \mathbf{K_g \sim 10^{123}}
\end{equation}
The introduction of $\mathbf{K_g}$ effectively imposes a $\mathbf{UV}$-filter, transforming quadratic divergences into stable dependencies. This foundational result establishes $K_g$ as the **universal self-tuning mechanism** of the $\text{TCFQ}$ vacuum.

\section{The Higgs Screening Mechanism}
\label{sec:higgs_screening}

In this section, we derive the central result of this paper: the dynamic suppression of the portal coupling $\mathbf{\lambda_{H\Phi}}$ arising from the constitutive nature of the vacuum.

\subsection{Constitutive Impedance Matching}
In the $\text{TCFQ}$ framework, energy scales are physically realized as tension densities within the constitutive field $\Phi$. The background vacuum, determined by the symmetry breaking scale $\mathbf{v \sim 10^{17} \, \text{GeV}}$, possesses an extremely high **Constitutive Impedance** ($\mathbf{Z_{\Phi}}$), while the electroweak sector, characterized by $\mathbf{v_{EW} \sim 246 \, \text{GeV}}$, represents a vastly lower impedance ($\mathbf{Z_{H}}$).

The interaction coefficient $\mathbf{\lambda_{H\Phi}}$ between these two sectors is governed by a **Generalized Impedance Matching** condition. This condition ensures that the transfer of mass-energy from the rigid, high-scale background ($\Phi$) to the soft, low-scale Higgs ($H$) is suppressed by the mismatch of their characteristic scales. We propose that the physical portal coupling is:
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:impedance_matching}
\mathbf{\lambda_{H\Phi} \approx \lambda_0 \left( \frac{v_{EW}}{v} \right)^2}
\end{equation}
Using the established values, the physical coupling is naturally predicted to be:
\begin{equation}
\mathbf{\lambda_{H\Phi} \approx \left( \frac{2.46 \times 10^2}{10^{17}} \right)^2 \sim 10^{-30}}
\end{equation}
\textbf{This extreme suppression is a dynamical necessity. The vacuum acts as a quantum filter, enforcing this 'transfer ratio' to maintain stability.}

\subsection{Derivation of the Screened Mass Correction}
We now apply the derived constitutive coupling to the quadratic divergence of the Higgs mass. Consider the one-loop correction to the Higgs mass squared ($\mathbf{\Delta m_H^2}$) due to the scalar field $\Phi$. In the context of $\text{TCFQ}$, where the high-energy cutoff ($\mathbf{\Lambda_{cutoff}}$) is identified with the constitutive scale ($\mathbf{v}$), the correction is:
\begin{equation}
\mathbf{\Delta m_H^2 \approx \frac{\lambda_{H\Phi}}{16\pi^2} \Lambda_{cutoff}^2}
\end{equation}
Substituting the $\text{TCFQ}$ derived quantities ($\mathbf{\lambda_{H\Phi} \sim v_{EW}^2/v^2}$ and $\mathbf{\Lambda_{cutoff} \approx v}$):
\begin{equation}
\mathbf{\Delta m_H^2 \approx \frac{1}{16\pi^2} \left( \frac{v_{EW}^2}{\mathbf{v^2}} \right) \mathbf{v^2} = \frac{1}{16\pi^2} v_{EW}^2}
\end{equation}
\textbf{Crucially, the quadratic dependence on the high-energy scale cancels out exactly.} The resulting correction is naturally of the order of the $\text{electroweak}$ scale itself ($\mathbf{v_{EW}}$).

\subsection{The Higgs Screening Theorem}
We formalize this result as the **Higgs Screening Theorem**:
\textit{In the $\text{TCFQ}$ framework, the Universal Constitutive Modulus ($\mathbf{K_g}$) of the vacuum dynamically enforces a Generalized Impedance Matching condition that suppresses the portal coupling $\mathbf{\lambda_{H\Phi}}$. This suppression results in the exact cancellation of quadratic divergences, rendering the physical mass naturally stable.}

Mathematically, the physical mass $\mathbf{m_H^2}$ is expressed as:
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:mass_stabilization}
\mathbf{m_H^2 = (m_H^0)^2 + \delta m_{screened}^2 + \delta m_{log}^2}
\end{equation}
where $\mathbf{\delta m_{screened}^2 \sim v_{EW}^2}$ is finite and natural.

\begin{equation}
\mathbf{\mathcal{L}_{\chi-gauge} = 0} \quad (\text{at tree level})
\end{equation}
This implies that the fasón **does not interact with photons or gluons** ($T^{\mu}_{\mu} = 0$). Consequently, it evades constraints from optical experiments and precision QED tests.

The TCFQ framework is highly falsifiable, offering two distinct experimental avenues for refutation:

    \begin{equation}
    \mathbf{\text{Prediction 1: Coherent Dark Matter Modulation Signal at } \sim 100 \, \text{MHz}}

    \textit{Refutation Condition:} If future low-energy precision experiments (e.g., next-generation axion/DM detectors) \textbf{fail to detect} this specific signal, the TCFQ's self-tuning mechanism for $\Lambda$ is refuted.

    \item \textbf{Electroweak Sector ($m_H$):} The Higgs Screening mechanism is entirely dependent on the dynamical enforcement of the ultra-suppressed portal coupling:
    \begin{equation}
    \mathbf{\text{Prediction 2: Portal Coupling } \lambda_{H\Phi} \lesssim 10^{-30}}

    \textit{Refutation Condition:} If future high-precision measurements of Higgs properties or searches for new physics \textbf{detect a portal coupling} $\lambda_{H\Phi}$ significantly larger than $10^{-30}$, the Higgs Screening Theorem is invalidated.
